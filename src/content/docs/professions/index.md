Ultima Online has no classes — but players still talk in **professions**: "I'm a tamer,"
"she's a smith," "he runs a treasure hunter." A profession is a cluster of skills, a
playstyle, and a way of making a living. This section is the **by-profession view of the
wiki**: each page is a hub that pulls together everything for one career — the skills it
uses, the [build template](/templates/) to get there, the [how-to guides](/playing/) for
its day-to-day, and the items and tools of the trade.

It's the answer to "I want to *be* a ___ — where do I start and what do I need?"

:::tip[Professions vs. Templates vs. Skills]
- **Professions** (here) — the career overview: what the job is and links to everything for it.
- **[Character Templates](/templates/)** — the detailed *build + progression storyline* (stats, skill order, where to hunt at each stage).
- **[Skills](/skills/)** — the reference page for one individual skill.
:::

## Player-vs-player

Fighting other players is its own discipline, with its own builds — the tank mage, the
nox mage, the stun mage, the dexxer, the stealth archer. See **[PvP Builds](/professions/pvp/)**
for the classic player-killer and duelist templates and how they differ from PvM characters
(and [Notoriety & PvP](/playing/notoriety-and-pvp/) for the rules of engagement).

## You are a blend

Most players are a **blend** — a tamer-mage, a warrior-smith, a stealth archer, a
[sampire](/professions/sampire/). The 700-point [skill cap](/mechanics/skill-gain/) (seven
Grandmaster skills, see [7x GM Templates](/templates/seven-gm/)) is what forces the
interesting choices.
