## What this profession is

The necromancer is a dark caster: it curses enemies, raises and commands the dead,
and transforms its own body for combat advantages. On this shard Necromancy is real
(the EJ expansion stack includes Age-of-Shadows-era skills). It pairs naturally with a
mage (for [Magery](/skills/magery/) utility) or with a weapon and Bushido — the latter is
the famous [sampire](/professions/sampire/).

## Core skills

- [Necromancy](/skills/necromancy/) — the casting skill; sets spell power, success, and which spells you can use.
- [Spirit Speak](/skills/spirit-speak/) — boosts your Necromancy effects **and** lets you drain life from nearby corpses to heal yourself. A necromancer without Spirit Speak is half-powered.
- A secondary: [Magery](/skills/magery/) + [Evaluating Intelligence](/skills/evaluating-intelligence/) for a full caster, **or** a weapon skill ([Swordsmanship](/skills/swordsmanship/)/etc.) + [Tactics](/skills/tactics/)/[Anatomy](/skills/anatomy/) for a dexxer hybrid.
- [Meditation](/skills/meditation/) (caster builds) and [Resisting Spells](/skills/resisting-spells/) round it out.

## Special reagents, not the eight

Necromancy does **not** use the eight mage reagents. It consumes its own set: **bat wing,
grave dust, daemon blood, nox crystal, and pig iron**. Buy them from mage/reagent vendors and
keep a deep stock — each spell lists its own combination. (Confirmed in the spell files under
`servuo: Scripts/Spells/Necromancy/`.) Mana, not tithing, pays for the cast on top of regs.

## The build

There is **no dedicated necromancer template page** yet. For a caster necromancer, start from
the [Mage Template](/templates/mage/) and swap in Necromancy + Spirit Speak alongside Magery +
Eval + Meditation + Resist. For a melee necromancer, start from the [Warrior Template](/templates/warrior/)
and fit Necromancy + Spirit Speak around the weapon core. Either way, see
[7x GM Templates](/templates/seven-gm/) for squeezing it all under the 700-point cap.

## How to play it

Read [Magic Schools](/playing/magic-schools/) and [Spellcasting](/playing/spellcasting/) for
the casting model, and [Meditation & Mana](/playing/meditation-and-mana/) for mana recovery.
Melee hybrids should also read [Combat Advanced](/playing/combat-advanced/).

The toolkit (all confirmed under `servuo: Scripts/Spells/Necromancy/`):

- **Forms** — *Wraith Form* (mana-leech, wraith body), *Lich Form* (mana regen, weakness to fire), and *Horrific Beast* (a melee body). *Vampiric Embrace* gives **life-leech on every hit** — the cornerstone of the sampire.
- **Curses** — *Corpse Skin*, *Blood Oath*, *Strangle*, *Pain Spike*, *Wither* (area) weaken and bleed targets.
- **Undead** — *Animate Dead* raises corpses into servants; *Summon Familiar* gives a personal pet.
- **Healing** — drain life off corpses with [Spirit Speak](/skills/spirit-speak/) between fights.

## Gear

- [Magic item properties](/magic/) — caster necros want **Lower Reagent Cost**, **Lower Mana Cost**, **Spell Damage Increase**, and **Faster Casting**; melee necros want hit-leech and swing speed instead.
- [Armor](/items/armor/) — balanced resists; some forms override your appearance and stats.
- [Weapons](/items/weapons/) for hybrid builds. Carry a full set of the five special reagents.

## Making a living

Necromancers farm dungeons solo: curse-and-nuke a target, heal off the bodies it leaves, and
loot the corpses. Undead-heavy and high-tier content rewards the curse kit well. Sell surplus
via [Vendors & Banking](/playing/vendors-and-banking/).
