Most of this wiki is **reference**: tables of skill caps, spell circles, monster
stats, crafting recipes — the kind of pages you check mid-fight and then close.
This section is the other thing entirely. These are the pages you read by a campfire
in West Britain bank, the long-form, opinionated, player-voice writing that the old
Stratics columns and UO forum threads did so well. Less "what is the formula for
Eval Int," more "here is how to *be* a mage, and why it will break your heart and
then save your life."

We keep them separate on purpose. A reference page wants to be terse, sourced, and
right. An essay wants to be warm, argumentative, and *true* in the way a good piece
of advice from a veteran is true even when it skips the footnotes. That is why every
page here carries `status: unverified` — not because it is wrong, but because it is
**opinion and craft**, not extracted game data. Take it as the voice of someone who
has played the character and wants you to love it too.

## Two kinds of writing live here

**Essays** — long-form how-to and philosophy. Original pieces written for this wiki,
in the spirit of the classic UO guide-essays. They take a subject the reference pages
cover in fragments and tell the whole story start to finish: the philosophy of the
build, the grind, the economy, the playstyle, the mistakes, the endgame.

- [Building a Mage](/essays/building-a-mage/) — the complete life of a mage, from
  your first Magic Arrow to the day you Recall out of a gank with a sliver of mana left.
- [The Treasure Hunter's Handbook](/essays/treasure-hunters-handbook/) — the romance
  and the reality of digging up chests guarded by things that want you dead.
- [Surviving Your First Week](/essays/surviving-your-first-week/) — a warm letter to
  the brand-new player: the first hour, the first gold, the first death, and the traps
  that send people quitting before the game even starts.

**Tales & Resources** — the rest of the world's best UO writing, gathered and pointed
to. Short summaries of community stories in our own words, plus an annotated list of
the great external guides, official and fan-made. We do not copy these; we celebrate
them and send you to the source.

- [Tales & Resources](/essays/tales-and-resources/) — stories from Britannia, and a
  curated reading list of the guides every player should know about.

## How to read this section

If you are new, start with [Surviving Your First Week](/essays/surviving-your-first-week/),
then pick a path and read its essay. If you already know your way around Britannia,
the essays are still worth your time — they are written by and for people who have
been ganked, gone broke, lost a tamed dragon to a lag spike, and stayed anyway.

When an essay makes a factual claim you want to nail down, follow its links into the
reference tree — every essay cross-links the [skills](/skills/), [professions](/professions/),
[magic](/magic/), and [playing](/playing/) pages that back it up. The essay gives you
the *why*; the reference gives you the *exactly how much*.

## A note on the sources we summarize

The UO community has been writing brilliant guides for over twenty-five years.
Some of those writers are why this section exists. Where we summarize or recommend
their work — Stratics columns, master forum guides, official PDFs — we do so in our
own words and link out with attribution. Their pages are theirs. Go read them; then
come back and tell us what we got wrong in a report.
