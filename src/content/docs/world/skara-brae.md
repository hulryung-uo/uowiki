Skara Brae, the City of Spirituality, sits on a small island off the western coast — a quiet,
green town of rangers, ranchers, and bards where the loudest sound is usually livestock. It is
the spiritual and pastoral heart of Britannia: a ferry ride from the mainland, ringed by
farmland, and home to the ranger guild that watches the western wilds. Tamers come for the
fields full of beasts to charm, rangers for the woods to track, and everyone else for the
unhurried pace. The Virtue of Spirituality suits it — this is a town that feels like a deep
breath.

## Getting there

Skara Brae is an island off the **west** coast, reached by ferry from the mainland. The
**Skara Brae Moongate** (643, 2067) is on the mainland side just north of the island — take
any public gate, choose Skara Brae, and catch the ferry across. Mages can
[*Recall*](/skills/magery/) to a marked rune on the island itself. See
[Moongates & shrines](/world/moongates-and-shrines/).

## Who should come here

**Tamers, rangers, and bards.** The animal trainer, the taming fields, and the stables make
Skara Brae the tamer's traditional first home. Rangers train tracking in the eastern woods,
and the town's pace suits anyone who prefers their adventure at a remove. The Shrine of
Spirituality lies east on the mainland.
