Nujel'm is Britannia's pleasure capital — an opulent island city of marble, gardens, and
leisure in the eastern sea. Where most towns are built around a trade or a Virtue, Nujel'm is
built around **luxury**: a grand **palace**, a famous **casino**, and the easy wealth of a
place that exists to be enjoyed. It has the air of a resort, sunny and a little decadent, and
its jeweler and fine-goods shops reflect a clientele with coin to spend. Reached by its own
gate and by sea, Nujel'm is a change of pace from the working towns of the mainland.

## Getting there

- **Center:** (3770, 1170) — an island in the **east**, south of [Moonglow](/world/moonglow/)
- There is **no public Nujel'm moongate**; reach the island by **boat**, or by
  [*Recall*](/skills/magery/) / *Gate Travel* to a marked rune. See
  [Moongates & shrines](/world/moongates-and-shrines/) for the nearest gates (Moonglow lies
  north).

The **jeweler** and fine-clothing trades suit Nujel'm's wealthy character, with a mapmaker and
shipwright for the docks, plus a bowyer, butcher, fur trader, tanner, weaver, and a bard guild
master. Lore adds a grand **palace** and a famous **casino** to the island's attractions, though
these are atmosphere rather than functional buildings on our shard. Note that — unlike the
mainland cities — Nujel'm has no general healer or mage shop among its town vendors; stock up
before you visit.
