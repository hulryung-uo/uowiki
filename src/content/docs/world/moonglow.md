On the island of Verity in the far east stands Moonglow, the City of Honesty — Britannia's
great seat of magic and learning. This is the mages' town: scholars argue spell theory in the
streets, reagents grow in the island's fields, and the grandest building is not a castle or a
keep but the **Lycaeum**, the realm's foremost library and academy of the arcane. Honesty is
its Virtue, study its pastime. Remote and a little rarefied, Moonglow rewards anyone who comes
to master [Magery](/skills/magery/) with everything the craft demands, all within a short walk.

## Getting there

Moonglow sits on its island in the **far east** — the **Moonglow Moongate** (4467, 1283) is the
practical way in, since the island is otherwise reachable only by boat. Take any public gate and
choose Moonglow. Mages, fittingly, can [*Recall*](/skills/magery/) or *Gate* straight here. See
[Moongates & shrines](/world/moongates-and-shrines/).

The [mage shop](/professions/mage/) and **alchemist/herbalist** (your
[reagents](/items/reagents/) and potions) are the draw; a mapmaker and shipwright serve the
coast. Landmarks: the **Lycaeum** (4456, 1150), Britannia's great library and academy of magic,
and the **lighthouse** on the southern coast. The undead dungeon **Deceit** is on a separate
island to the north.

## Who should come here

**Mages, scribes, and alchemists — this is your city.** Mage shops for reagents and spell
scrolls, scribes for inscription supplies, alchemists for bottles, and the bank a short walk
from all of them. Training [Magery](/skills/magery/) here means never running dry of
reagents. The moongate makes the island less isolated than it looks on the map.
