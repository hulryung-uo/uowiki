Minoc, the City of Sacrifice, smells of coal smoke and honest work. Tucked against the
mountains of the north-east, it is the realm's mining and tinkering town: a place built on
ore and elbow grease, where miners and smiths outnumber everyone else and the ring of hammers
carries down the streets. Minoc gave its name to the Virtue of Sacrifice, and the town wears
its plain, working-class character with pride — no palaces here, just forges, carts, and the
mountainside that feeds them.

## Getting there

Minoc is in the **north-east** of the Felucca/Trammel mainland. The **Minoc Moongate**
(2701, 692) sits just south-east of town and also serves nearby [Vesper](/world/vesper/) — take
any public gate and choose Minoc. By road, Minoc connects south-west toward Britain and
south-east to Vesper; mages can [*Recall*](/skills/magery/) to a marked rune. See
[Moongates & shrines](/world/moongates-and-shrines/).

spawns in town. For tailoring, jewelry, or a mage shop, Vesper is a short hop east.

- **Forest to the south** for [lumberjacking](/skills/lumberjacking/).

## Who should come here

**Miners and smiths, first and foremost.** If you are training [Mining](/skills/mining/) or
[Blacksmithy](/skills/blacksmithy/), Minoc is your home base: mountainside, forge, smith
vendors, and bank in a tight loop. Tinkers also start well here (tools and ingots on hand).
Nearby Vesper handles anything Minoc's small vendor roster lacks.
