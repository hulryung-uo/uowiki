Buccaneer's Den is the one town in Britannia where the guards never come. A pirate haven hidden
on an island in the central sea, it is home to thieves, smugglers, and anyone with reason to
avoid the law. The catch — and the appeal — is right there in the rules: **call "guards" here
and no one answers.** That makes the Den genuinely dangerous, especially in [Felucca](/world/),
but it also makes it a hub for the gray-market trades that polite cities won't host. Come for
the thieves' guild and the special vendors; stay alert, because everyone else came for the same
reasons you did.

## Getting there

- **Center:** (2705, 2100) — an island in the **central** sea, south of [Cove](/world/cove/)
- **Buccaneer's Den Moongate** (2711, 2234) sits just south of town — but remember, **the gate
  drops you somewhere with no guards beyond it**. Boats also dock here. Mages can
  [*Recall*](/skills/magery/) to a marked rune. See
  [Moongates & shrines](/world/moongates-and-shrines/).

:::caution[No guards]
Unlike every other town listed here, Buccaneer's Den is **unguarded**. Players can attack you in
the open on Felucca, and calling for guards does nothing. Carry only what you can afford to lose.
:::

in one piece is the hard part.
