The **High Seas** content opens up the water: buy a ship, sail the coasts and deep ocean,
**fish** for far more than the shallows offer, fire **cannons** at enemy ships and sea
monsters, and hunt the great **sea bosses**. It's a whole second world layered over Britannia's
oceans.

## Ships

You place a ship from a **ship deed** onto open water. The classic boats are joined by full
**galleons** (`BaseGalleon`) in several styles:

- **Rowboat** — the small starter craft.
- **Britannian, Gargish, Orcish, and Tokuno galleons** — large multi-deck ships with a hold,
  cannons, and a pilot, each with its own look.

You steer from the **ship's wheel / tiller** (or order the **galleon pilot**), and use a
**mooring line** to dock. **Ship runes** and **ship tracking** help you mark and find your
vessel, and a **dock master** handles ships in port. Galleons can be **painted** and carry a
**hold** (`GalleonHold`) for cargo.

## Deep-sea fishing

Out past the shallows, **fishing** ([Fishing](/skills/fishing/) skill) yields the good stuff:
big fish, special catches, message-in-a-bottle treasure maps, and crafting fish — using a
**fishing pole**, **bait**, and nets. Sea charts and the right gear turn fishing from a
downtime hobby into a real income loop.

## Cannons and ship combat

Galleons mount **cannons** (`Cannon`) loaded with **cannonballs** and powder (built from
**cannon deeds**). You can fire on:

- **Other ships** in open-PvP waters, and
- **Sea monsters** that attack your vessel.

Crewing the cannons during a sea fight is the naval side of combat — aim, load, and broadside.

## Sea bosses

The ocean has its own roster of bosses worth sailing for:

- **Charybdis** — a colossal sea horror summoned with a fishing-related lure.
- **Corgul the Soulbinder** — boss of a remote ocean island.
- **Osiredon the Scalis Enforcer** — a deep-sea peerless-class threat.

These reward nautical-themed loot and are a step up from anything you'll snag on a fishing line.
